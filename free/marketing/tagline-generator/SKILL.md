# Tagline Generator

## Overview

Create taglines that become synonymous with your brand. This skill helps you craft memorable slogans that distill your brand promise into a few powerful words that resonate and stick.

## When to Use

- Developing brand taglines for new businesses
- Creating campaign-specific slogans
- Refreshing outdated taglines
- Generating product-specific catchphrases
- Developing event or initiative taglines

## Output Structure

```
BRAND/PRODUCT: [Name]
CORE VALUE PROPOSITION: [What makes it valuable]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [Who it's for]
BRAND PERSONALITY: [Tone/character traits]

---

TAGLINE OPTIONS:

Category 1: Benefit-Focused
1. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]
2. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]

Category 2: Emotional/Aspirational
3. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]
4. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]

Category 3: Clever/Memorable
5. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]
6. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]

Category 4: Action-Oriented
7. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]
8. "[Tagline]" - [Why it works]

---

TOP RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. [Best overall]: "[Tagline]"
   - Best for: [Use case/context]
2. [Runner-up]: "[Tagline]"
   - Best for: [Use case/context]

PAIRING SUGGESTIONS:
- Logo lockup: [How it pairs with brand name]
- Hashtag version: #[CampaignTag]
```

## Quick Reference

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| Length | 3-7 words ideal |
| Clarity | Understandable instantly |
| Uniqueness | Not used by competitors |
| Relevance | True to brand promise |
| Memorability | Easy to recall and repeat |
| Timelessness | Avoids dated references |

## Tagline Techniques

### Alliteration
Using same starting sounds:
- "Best Buy"
- "PayPal"

### Rhyme
Creating rhythmic flow:
- "Finger lickin' good"
- "See the USA in your Chevrolet"

### Parallelism
Balanced structure:
- "Easy to use. Hard to resist."
- "Eat Fresh."

### Contrast
Unexpected juxtaposition:
- "Think Different"
- "Impossible is Nothing"

### Metaphor
Symbolic meaning:
- "Red Bull gives you wings"
- "The Ultimate Driving Machine"

### Command
Direct action:
- "Just Do It"
- "Think Different"

## Tagline Categories

### Descriptive
What you do or offer:
- "The world's local bank" (HSBC)

### Superlative
Best-in-class claim:
- "The Ultimate Driving Machine" (BMW)

### Imperative
Call to action:
- "Just Do It" (Nike)

### Provocative
Challenge thinking:
- "Think Different" (Apple)

### Emotional
Feeling-focused:
- "Because you're worth it" (L'Oreal)

### Clever/Playful
Wit and wordplay:
- "Every kiss begins with Kay" (Kay Jewelers)

## Common Mistakes

- **Too generic**: "Quality you can trust" = everyone
- **Too long**: More than 7 words loses memorability
- **Jargon**: Industry terms don't resonate
- **Competitor echoes**: Sounds like existing slogans
- **Feature focus**: Benefits beat features
- **Temporary references**: Dates quickly
- **Internal focus**: About you, not customer

## Examples

### Example 1: Tech Startup Tagline

**Input**: Create taglines for a productivity app that helps remote teams stay organized

**Output**:
```
BRAND/PRODUCT: TeamSync (productivity app)
CORE VALUE PROPOSITION: Keeps remote teams organized and aligned
TARGET AUDIENCE: Remote-first companies, distributed teams
BRAND PERSONALITY: Modern, efficient, friendly, reliable

---

TAGLINE OPTIONS:

Category 1: Benefit-Focused
1. "Remote teams, one place." - Simple, clear value prop, parallel structure
2. "Finally, everyone's on the same page." - Addresses the pain point directly

Category 2: Emotional/Aspirational
3. "Distance doesn't have to feel distant." - Emotional connection, contrast
4. "Where teamwork happens." - Aspirational, positioning as the hub

Category 3: Clever/Memorable
5. "Stay together while apart." - Paradox creates memorability
6. "The office you carry with you." - Metaphor, portability implied

Category 4: Action-Oriented
7. "Sync up. Ship faster." - Action + outcome, parallel
8. "Work together, from anywhere." - Permission statement, inclusive

---

TOP RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. [Best overall]: "Stay together while apart."
   - Best for: Brand tagline, emotional resonance, unique positioning
2. [Runner-up]: "Sync up. Ship faster."
   - Best for: Performance marketing, action-oriented contexts

PAIRING SUGGESTIONS:
- Logo lockup: "TeamSync: Stay together while apart."
- Hashtag version: #StayTogetherApart
```

```

2. "Coffee worth waking up for." - Universal morning ritual, quality implied

Category 2: Emotional/Aspirational
3. "Every cup tells a story." - Emotional connection to sourcing
4. "Good coffee. Good conscience." - Dual benefit, parallel structure

Category 3: Clever/Memorable
5. "Roasted with care. Sourced with purpose." - Parallel, dual message
6. "Better beans. Better mornings." - Simple parallel, benefit-focused

Category 4: Action-Oriented
7. "Taste the difference ethics make." - Challenge, differentiator
8. "Start your day with purpose." - Lifestyle positioning

---

TOP RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. [Best overall]: "Good coffee. Good conscience."
   - Best for: Brand tagline, captures both quality and ethics simply
2. [Runner-up]: "Every cup tells a story."

```

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] 7 words or fewer
- [ ] Instantly understandable
- [ ] Memorable (say it once, remember later)
- [ ] True to brand promise
- [ ] Unique (not used by competitors)
- [ ] Works across contexts (ads, website, packaging)
- [ ] Sounds good spoken aloud
- [ ] Not dated by references
- [ ] Focuses on customer benefit
- [ ] Passes the "competitor test" (would this work for anyone?)

# Case Study Writer

## Overview

Create compelling customer success stories that demonstrate your product or service value through real results. This skill helps you structure case studies with narrative flow, specific metrics, and persuasive proof that turns prospects into customers.

## When to Use

- Showcasing customer success stories
- Creating sales enablement content
- Building proof for landing pages
- Developing testimonial content
- Writing client spotlights
- Creating before/after transformation stories

## Output Structure

```
CASE STUDY OVERVIEW:
- Customer: [Company/individual name]
- Industry: [Their industry]
- Challenge: [One-line problem summary]
- Solution: [Your product/service used]
- Key Result: [Most impressive metric]

---

HEADLINE: [Result-focused, attention-grabbing title]

SUBHEADLINE: [Context or additional detail]

---

CUSTOMER SNAPSHOT:
- Company: [Name]
- Industry: [Industry]
- Size: [Employees/revenue/users]
- Location: [Where they're based]
- Product Used: [Your specific offering]

---

THE CHALLENGE:

[2-3 paragraphs describing their situation before]
- What problem were they facing?
- What had they already tried?
- What was at stake?

PULL QUOTE: "[Powerful quote about the pain point]" — [Name, Title]

---

THE SOLUTION:

[2-3 paragraphs on how they discovered and implemented your solution]
- Why did they choose you?
- How was the implementation?
- What was the experience like?

PULL QUOTE: "[Quote about why they chose you or implementation experience]" — [Name, Title]

---

THE RESULTS:

[Paragraph summarizing outcomes]

KEY METRICS:
- [Metric 1]: [Before] → [After] ([% improvement])
- [Metric 2]: [Before] → [After] ([% improvement])
- [Metric 3]: [Specific result or achievement]

PULL QUOTE: "[Quote about results]" — [Name, Title]

---

LOOKING AHEAD:

[Brief paragraph on future plans, continued partnership, or next steps]

---

CALL TO ACTION:

[Invitation to learn more or get similar results]

BUTTON: [CTA text]
```

## Quick Reference

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| Headline | Lead with the result, include numbers |
| Customer quote | Use throughout, makes it real |
| Metrics | Before/after with % improvement |
| Challenge | Be specific about the pain |
| Solution | Focus on experience, not features |
| Length | 500-1000 words for written case studies |
| Format | Scannable with headers and bullets |
| Visuals | Include company logo, headshot if possible |

## Case Study Formats

### The Narrative
- Tells a story from start to finish
- Emotional journey emphasis
- Good for: Complex transformations

### The Quick Win
- Short, punchy, metric-focused
- Skimmable format
- Good for: Sales decks, landing pages

### The Deep Dive
- Detailed implementation story
- Technical specifics included
- Good for: B2B, enterprise sales

### The Video Script
- Interview-based structure
- Conversational quotes
- Good for: Video testimonials

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Headline leads with specific, impressive result
- [ ] Customer is relatable to target audience
- [ ] Challenge section describes real, specific pain
- [ ] Solution section focuses on experience, not features
- [ ] Results include specific metrics with before/after
- [ ] Customer quotes appear throughout
- [ ] Story has clear narrative arc (challenge → solution → results)
- [ ] Call-to-action connects to reader's situation
- [ ] Avoids generic praise ("great service")
- [ ] Includes enough detail to be credible
- [ ] Scannable format with clear headers

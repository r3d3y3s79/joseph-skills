# Headline Generator

## Overview

Your headline is the most important thing you write. 80% of people read the headline; only 20% read the rest. This skill helps you generate multiple headline options using tested formulas that capture attention, create curiosity, and drive clicks - without resorting to manipulation or clickbait.

## When to Use

- Writing blog post or article titles
- Creating YouTube video titles
- Crafting email subject lines
- Writing ad headlines
- Naming products or courses
- Creating social media hooks
- A/B testing existing headlines

## Output Structure

```
CONTENT SUMMARY: [One sentence describing the content]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [Who this is for]
CONTENT TYPE: [Blog/Video/Email/Ad/Product]
PRIMARY KEYWORD: [If SEO relevant]

---

HEADLINE OPTIONS:

CURIOSITY-DRIVEN:
1. [Headline using information gap]
2. [Headline with unexpected element]
3. [Headline posing a question]

BENEFIT-FOCUSED:
4. [Headline emphasizing outcome]
5. [Headline with specific result]
6. [Headline addressing pain point]

FORMULA-BASED:
7. [How to + benefit]
8. [Number + topic + outcome]
9. [Challenge common belief]

---

TOP RECOMMENDATION: [Best headline with reasoning]

A/B TEST SUGGESTION:
- Version A: [Headline 1]
- Version B: [Headline 2]
- Why: [What you're testing]
```

## Quick Reference

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|--------------|
| Length | 6-12 words (55-70 characters for SEO) |
| Numbers | Odd numbers outperform even |
| Power words | Free, new, instantly, proven, secret |
| First words | "How to", numbers, "Why", questions |
| Specificity | Specific beats vague every time |
| Clarity vs. clever | Clarity wins |
| Promise | Make one you can keep |

## Headline Formulas

### The "How To"
`How to [achieve outcome] (without [pain point])`
- How to Write Faster Without Sacrificing Quality
- How to Double Your Email Open Rate in 30 Days

### The Number List
`[Number] [things] that [outcome]`
- 7 Morning Habits That Changed My Productivity
- 13 Pricing Mistakes That Kill Conversions

### The Question
`[Intriguing question that reader wants answered]`
- Why Do Most Productivity Systems Fail?
- What Happens When You Stop Checking Email?

### The Contrarian
`[Common belief] is Wrong. Here's Why.`
- Everything You Know About SEO is Wrong
- Stop Setting Goals (Do This Instead)

### The Case Study
`How [Person/Company] [achieved result] in [timeframe]`
- How Shopify Grew to $1B Without Traditional Ads
- How I Built 10K Followers in 90 Days

### The Fear of Missing Out
`[Number] [things] You're Missing in [topic]`
- 5 LinkedIn Features You're Probably Not Using
- The Google Ads Setting You're Ignoring

### The Mistake
`[Number] [topic] Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)`
- 9 Landing Page Mistakes Killing Your Conversions
- The Resume Mistake That's Costing You Interviews

### The Specific Result
`[Achieve exact result] in [specific timeframe]`
- Double Your Writing Speed in 2 Weeks
- Get Your First 1,000 Subscribers in 60 Days

## Common Mistakes

- **Too clever**: Puns and wordplay usually fail
- **Vague promises**: "Be more successful" means nothing
- **Clickbait lies**: Promise what you deliver, deliver what you promise
- **Too long**: If it wraps on mobile, it's too long
- **Weak first words**: Front-load the interesting part
- **No benefit**: What's in it for the reader?
- **Jargon**: Use words your audience uses

## Examples

### Example 1: Blog Post Headlines

**Input**: Generate headlines for a blog post about email marketing for small businesses

**Output**:
```
CONTENT SUMMARY: Comprehensive guide to email marketing strategies for small businesses
TARGET AUDIENCE: Small business owners, 1-10 employees
CONTENT TYPE: Blog post
PRIMARY KEYWORD: email marketing for small business

---

HEADLINE OPTIONS:

CURIOSITY-DRIVEN:
1. The Email Strategy That's Working for Small Businesses Right Now
2. Why Your Email List Isn't Growing (And What to Do Instead)
3. What I Learned Sending 10,000 Emails for a Small Business

BENEFIT-FOCUSED:
4. Email Marketing for Small Business: Get More Sales Without More Work
5. How to Build an Email List That Actually Buys
6. Turn Your Email List into Your Best Sales Channel

FORMULA-BASED:
7. How to Start Email Marketing for Your Small Business (Complete Guide)
8. 9 Email Marketing Strategies That Work for Small Businesses
9. Forget Social Media. Email is Where Small Businesses Win.

---

TOP RECOMMENDATION: "9 Email Marketing Strategies That Work for Small Businesses"

Reasoning: Includes primary keyword naturally, promises specific value (9 strategies), qualifies audience (small businesses), and sets clear expectations. Works for SEO while being compelling to click.

A/B TEST SUGGESTION:
- Version A: 9 Email Marketing Strategies That Work for Small Businesses
- Version B: How to Build an Email List That Actually Buys
- Why: Testing specific tactics (listicle) vs. outcome-focused (how to with benefit). Will reveal if audience wants tactics or results framing.
```

### Example 2: YouTube Video Titles

**Input**: Generate titles for a YouTube video about my experience quitting coffee for 30 days

**Output**:
```
CONTENT SUMMARY: Personal experiment quitting coffee for 30 days, documenting effects
TARGET AUDIENCE: Productivity/self-improvement viewers, coffee drinkers curious about quitting
CONTENT TYPE: YouTube video
PRIMARY KEYWORD: quit coffee

---

HEADLINE OPTIONS:

CURIOSITY-DRIVEN:
1. I Quit Coffee for 30 Days. Here's What Happened.
2. The Truth About Quitting Coffee (30-Day Experiment)
3. Why I'll Never Drink Coffee Again (or Will I?)

BENEFIT-FOCUSED:
4. I Quit Coffee and My Productivity Doubled
5. What Happens to Your Body When You Quit Coffee
6. 30 Days Without Coffee: Sleep, Energy, and Mood Changes

FORMULA-BASED:
7. How to Quit Coffee Without the Withdrawal Hell
8. 7 Unexpected Things That Happened When I Quit Coffee
9. Everything They Don't Tell You About Quitting Coffee

---

TOP RECOMMENDATION: "I Quit Coffee for 30 Days. Here's What Happened."

Reasoning: YouTube rewards curiosity-driven titles. This is personal (I), specific (30 days), and creates an information gap (what happened?). Short enough to display fully on mobile. Classic experiment format that performs well.

A/B TEST SUGGESTION:
- Version A: I Quit Coffee for 30 Days. Here's What Happened.
- Version B: 7 Unexpected Things That Happened When I Quit Coffee
- Why: Testing pure curiosity vs. numbered list format. Both work on YouTube, but different algorithms may favor one.
```

### Example 3: Email Subject Lines

**Input**: Generate subject lines for a promotional email about a new online course launch

**Output**:
```
CONTENT SUMMARY: Launch email for new course on freelance writing
TARGET AUDIENCE: Aspiring freelance writers, career changers
CONTENT TYPE: Promotional email
PRIMARY KEYWORD: freelance writing course

---

HEADLINE OPTIONS:

CURIOSITY-DRIVEN:
1. I built something for you
2. This is different (here's why)
3. The thing I've been hinting at...

BENEFIT-FOCUSED:
4. Your freelance writing career starts here
5. Land your first paid writing client in 30 days
6. From hobbyist to paid writer (new course)

FORMULA-BASED:
7. [Announcement] Freelance Writing Launchpad is live
8. The freelance writing course I wish existed 5 years ago
9. 50% off ends Friday - Freelance Writing Launchpad

URGENCY/SCARCITY (use sparingly, must be true):
10. Early bird pricing ends tonight
11. Only 50 spots available
12. First 100 get [bonus]

---

TOP RECOMMENDATION: "Land your first paid writing client in 30 days"

Reasoning: Specific outcome (paid client), specific timeframe (30 days), speaks to their actual goal. Doesn't mention "course" in subject - saves that for email body. Creates curiosity about how.

A/B TEST SUGGESTION:
- Version A: Land your first paid writing client in 30 days
- Version B: I built something for you
- Why: Testing specific benefit vs. personal/curiosity approach. Reveals whether audience responds better to directness or relationship-based copy.

SEQUENCE SUGGESTION:
- Email 1: I built something for you (curiosity)
- Email 2: Land your first paid writing client in 30 days (benefit)
- Email 3: Early bird pricing ends tonight (urgency)
```

## Power Words Reference

### Emotional Triggers
| Category | Words |
|----------|-------|
| Urgency | Now, today, instantly, immediately, fast |
| Exclusivity | Secret, insider, hidden, private, exclusive |
| Safety | Proven, guaranteed, tested, trusted, reliable |
| Value | Free, bonus, save, extra, discount |
| Curiosity | Truth, revealed, discover, surprising, unexpected |

### Number Guidelines
- Odd numbers outperform even (7 beats 6)
- Specific beats round (137 beats 100)
- Lower numbers for actionable tips (5-9)
- Higher numbers for listicles (21, 37, 101)

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Under 70 characters (for SEO and mobile)
- [ ] Front-loaded with interesting words
- [ ] Clear benefit or curiosity hook
- [ ] Specific (not vague or generic)
- [ ] Matches content (not clickbait)
- [ ] Uses audience's language
- [ ] Would YOU click this?
- [ ] Passes the "so what?" test
- [ ] Multiple options for A/B testing
- [ ] Primary keyword included (if SEO relevant)
